The University Grants Commission (UGC) is a statutory body of the Government of India, established under the UGC Act of 1956. It is responsible for the coordination, determination, and maintenance of standards of higher education in India. UGC plays a crucial role in overseeing universities and granting recognition to institutions across the country, ensuring a uniform standard of academic excellence. In the context of engineering colleges, UGC works in tandem with other regulatory bodies like the AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education) to oversee technical education. While AICTE is the main approval authority for engineering programs, UGC ensures that engineering institutions affiliated with universities or deemed universities maintain academic standards in line with national policies. It also facilitates funding, faculty development, and quality assurance through various initiatives and schemes. Moreover, UGC encourages research, innovation, and the adoption of outcome-based education in engineering institutions.
